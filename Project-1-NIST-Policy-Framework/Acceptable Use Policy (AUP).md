**1. Policy Objective**

**(The "why")**

The purpose of this policy is to define the acceptable use of Gulf Digital Trust Bank's information systems, network resources, and company-issued devices (e.g., laptops, mobile phones). These rules are designed to protect the bank's assets, maintain the security and integrity of our network, and ensure employees use technology professionally and legally.

**2. Scope**

**(The "who" and "what")**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary staff using any bank-owned equipment, software, or network resources, whether on bank premises or remotely.

**3. Policy Statements**

**(The "rules"â€”the "do's and don'ts")**

**Prohibited Activities:** Users must NOT use bank systems for any of the following:

**Illegal or Malicious Activity:** Engaging in any activity that is illegal under UAE law, including hacking, fraud, or transmitting malicious software (viruses, ransomware).

**Unauthorized Access:** Attempting to bypass security controls or gain access to data, systems, or accounts for which they are not authorized.

**Data Mishandling:** Copying "Strictly Confidential" or "Confidential" data to unapproved personal devices (e.g., USB drives, personal cloud storage like Google Drive, personal email).

**Unauthorized Software:** Installing any software, applications, or browser extensions on bank devices without explicit approval from the IT Department.

**Network Harm:** Participating in activities that could degrade network performance, such as crypto-mining or running unauthorized servers.

**Inappropriate Content:** Accessing, downloading, or distributing offensive, discriminatory, or harassing material.

**Personal Use:** All bank equipment is provided for business purposes. Limited and incidental personal use (e.g., brief personal email) is permitted but is not private and is subject to monitoring.

**Required Conduct (The "Do's"):**

**Protect Assets:** Users are responsible for the physical security of their issued devices. Laptops must never be left unattended in public.

**Lock Screens:** Users must lock their workstations (e.g., press Win+L or Ctrl+Cmd+Q) whenever they step away from their desks.

**Report Incidents:** All lost or stolen devices, or any suspected security incidents (e.g., a suspicious email), must be reported to the IT Help Desk immediately.

**Remote Access:** Connect to the bank's network only using the approved corporate Virtual Private Network (VPN) when working remotely.

**4. Roles and Responsibilities**

**(The "who does what")**

**IT Department:** Responsible for monitoring network traffic, managing software installations, and providing secure remote access solutions (VPN).

**Managers:** Responsible for ensuring their team members have read and understand this policy.

**All Users:** Responsible for reading, understanding, and complying with this policy. Ignorance of the policy is not an excuse for non-compliance.

**5. Compliance Monitoring**

**(The "how we check")**

Gulf Digital Trust Bank reserves the right to monitor all activity on its network and devices, including email, internet usage, and file access. There is no expectation of privacy on bank-owned equipment.

Automated tools will be used to block unauthorized websites and detect malicious software.

Violation of this AUP is a serious offense that may lead to disciplinary action, revocation of access, and/or termination of employment.
